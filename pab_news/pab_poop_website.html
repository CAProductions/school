What Scientist Have Found So Far:
The Bear-Cat is a mammal that usually produces 3-5 offsprings at a time. It has small, sharp claws used to easily catch fish and other prey, thin pointy wiskers, large cat-like eyes, pointy ears, and a large, furry body. The Bear-cat has the tail of a bear. Bear-Cats have a veriety of possible furr colors even though they all have the same colored skin. They are very affectionate towords other Bear-Cats that they know very well such as their parents, sibilings, and best friends. The are usually passive-aggressive to anything or anyone they don't recognize, but eeach one is different and some can be very agressive to everything and some can be extremely shy. Their favorite foods are fish, dogs, mice, and honey. Giving a Bear-Cat some honey and being very nice to it has been proven to increase their trust in you by at least 70%.
Bear-Cats are hard to domesticate. At birth, they have many needs and can't do anything without their parents for at least two years. They then grow up a bit, but still keep very tight bonds with their parents, and will go crazy if you put them into a location that they are not used to. This is why it is optimal for the Bear-Cat in question to be at least 3 1/3 years old but no more than 5 years old before relocating. You should also kindly let the Bear-Cat deem you worthy before attempting to take one home. It is also worth mentioning the amount of respect Bear-Cats expect. If you do not care for it and treat it like how you would treat a human, it will think you look down onto it and you might not be able to wake up tomorrow. It is also apperantly possible to communicate with a Bear-Cat if you form a deep enough bond with it.

The Problem

The life and existence of the Bear-Cat isn't only fun and games, though. Bear-Cats have one fear: saltwater. Scientist are still trying to explain this, but Bear-Cats are specifically terrified of salt water and will do whatever it takes to get away from it. That is why they were located at only the heart of the island, instead of all around it. They also have many unguided travels to the spirit world, which sometimes leaves the Bear-Cats trapped there forever.
